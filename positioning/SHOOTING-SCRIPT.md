# Assay — Pitch Video (full shooting script, ~3:30–4:00)

Locked elements: Louvre scream hook, cheap model named (Gemini Flash), Knight Capital data (verified, SEC), human tone, demo as overlay (no walkthrough), two objections answered, loss-led, name = Assay. Every shot is filmable in Paris with what we have. No fabricated stats.

## SCENE 1 — COLD OPEN: THE LOUVRE SCREAM (0:00–0:40) — the hook
**Location:** Louvre, in front of the glass pyramid. Real tourists around.
**Shot:** Wide, cinematic. Håkan sits on the steps hunched over his laptop, working hard, oblivious to the beauty and the crowd. Camera slowly pulls back to reveal the pyramid and the people.
**Narrator (deep, cinematic VO):**
> "This is Håkan. He built an AI agent to run the payments on his platform. To save money, he ran it on the cheap model, Gemini Flash. It read the invoices, sent the transfers, said done. Looked clean. He shipped it."
**Action:** Håkan's phone buzzes. He glances. His face drops.
**Narrator:** "Then the bank called."
**Action:** Håkan slowly stands among the tourists and SCREAMS, full volume. Heads turn.
**Håkan (screaming):** "IT SENT THE WRONG AMOUNTS FOR THREE WEEKS, AND IT NEVER TOLD ME!"
**On screen (title card slams in):** ASSAY

## SCENE 2 — THE PROBLEM + THE DATA (0:40–1:25)
**Shot:** Michelle to camera, calm, confident.
**Michelle:**
> "Cheap AI doesn't crash when it's wrong. It's confidently wrong, silently, and you find out after it's cost you. And this isn't new. In 2012, a trading firm's software sent the wrong orders for 45 minutes, because nothing checked that what went out matched what was entered. It cost them four hundred and sixty million dollars, and the company was gone."
**On screen:** "Knight Capital — $460M lost in 45 minutes (SEC, 2012)"
**Michelle:** "We just handed that exact job to AI agents. They fail the same way."

## SCENE 3 — WHAT IT IS (1:25–1:55) — demo footage starts as overlay
**Shot:** Håkan or Michelle to camera; the live demo plays as picture-in-picture / background overlay.
**Michelle:**
> "So we built Assay. It sits on top of whatever model you already use, and it checks the model's work against reality, the actual math, the actual records, not another AI's opinion. The moment a step is wrong, it catches it and fixes just that step. You keep the cheap model, and you can finally trust it."

## SCENE 4 — THE TWO OBJECTIONS (1:55–2:25)
**Shot:** Håkan to camera, casual, a little cheeky.
**Håkan:**
> "Two things people always ask. 'Isn't this just a router?' No. A router picks your model for you. We keep the model you chose, and catch it when it's wrong. 'Isn't the checker just another AI?' No, and that's the whole point. We run the real test, the real math. It can't be confidently wrong the way the model is."

## SCENE 5 — THE DEMO MOMENT (2:25–3:00) — overlay at full screen
**Shot:** the live demo fills the frame; Håkan narrates over it.
**Håkan:**
> "This is real, running right now. A cheap model just queued a wire ten times too big. Two hundred OK, no error, it would've sent. Assay checked the actual math, caught that exact step, and stopped it."
**On screen:** the step flips RED → fixed → GREEN.

## SCENE 6 — THE LOSS + CLOSE (3:00–3:35)
**Shot:** back to Michelle, then Håkan, confident, calm.
**Michelle:**
> "It's not about saving tokens. It's about the wire that doesn't go out wrong, the bug that never reaches your customer. We stop the expensive mistake, and you still pay cheap-model prices."
**Håkan:** "Two lines to plug in. Keep your own model, your own keys. Assay just makes sure it's actually right."
**Final frame:** ASSAY wordmark + "Keep your model. We make sure it's right."
