The independent reliability layer that catches your AI agent's silently-wrong steps — and fixes only the step that broke.

The problem

A cheap model queued a wire for $8.8M too much — and nothing errored.

It read a trade ticket, slipped a decimal on the notional, returned HTTP 200, and the wrong number flowed straight toward a wire.

  • Cheap models fail confidently and silently — the answer looks clean, nothing crashes.
  • Models can't catch themselves: blind self-correction lands at AUROC ~0.54 — barely a coin flip.
  • The wrong number flows into a wire, a trade, a board deck, a downstream model.
Why the obvious fixes fail

Frontier-everywhere is 20× the cost. A router throws away your control.

  • Run the big model on every step → pay 20× to dodge a problem on ~5% of steps.
  • Use a router → it replaces your model with a guess; you still can't tell when it's wrong.
  • We are not a router. You keep the model you chose. We verify it, per step, in the loop.
How it works

Trace → catch against ground truth → recover only the broken step.

cheap step→ ✕ reality check: 9,850,000 ≠ 985,000 (10× slip)→ ↺ retry w/ reason→ ↗ escalate to Anthropic→ ✓ verified
  • Checks against reality — runs the test, does the arithmetic, validates the schema. Not another AI's opinion.
  • Recovery ladder: retry cheap with the reason → escalate cross-provider → kill-switch a runaway step.
  • Honesty boundary: when it can't deterministically verify, it flags low-confidence — never fakes a green.
